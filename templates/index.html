How it works: A moving average convergence/divergence (MACD) is a popular technical indicator to help investors identify entry and exit points. This MACD line is calculated by subtracting the 21 day exponential moving average (EMA) from the 10 day EMA, buying when the oscillator approaches 0 from below and selling when the oscillator approaches from above. The MACD is calculated daily at market close. The profit per share is updated every trade pair (long+short).

RSI Stock Trading/Analysis for GOOG (Google)
How it works: The relative strength index (RSI) is a popular momentum oscillator which informs investors about bullish and bearish momentum. An asset is usually considered overbought when the RSI is above 70 and oversold when it is below 30. The RSI line crossing below the overbought line or above oversold line is often seen by traders as a signal to buy or sell. The RSI is calculated daily at market close. The profit per share is updated every trade pair (long+short).
